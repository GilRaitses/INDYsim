### Background

What started as an exercise has developed into what I believe is a publishable contribution, and I wanted to discuss this with you before taking any next steps.

### What I've Done

Building on the linear-nonlinear-Poisson (LNP) modeling approach from your 2015 eLife paper (Gepner et al.), I developed an **interpretable closed-form kernel** for the optogenetic response. Instead of fitting 12 raised-cosine basis weights, I parameterized the temporal filter as a **gamma-difference function**—the mathematical form expected from cascaded first-order neural processes.

Key findings:
- The 6-parameter analytic kernel achieves R² = 0.968 against the 12-basis reference
- Parameters map directly to biological timescales: τ₁ = 0.29 s (fast transduction), τ₂ = 3.81 s (slow adaptation)
- Extension to a 2×2 factorial design (12 experiments) reveals that **kernel shape is conserved** across conditions while **amplitude is modulated**—suggesting dissociation between temporal dynamics and gain control

I've also implemented a RUN/TURN trajectory simulator driven by the fitted hazard model, which reproduces observed turn rates (1.88 vs 1.84 turns/min).

3. **Feedback**: If you have concerns, alternative suggestions, or would prefer a different arrangement, I'm very open to discussion.

## Notes on Tone and Approach

### Do:
- Be respectful and professional
- Acknowledge their contributions clearly
- Be flexible and open to negotiation
- Give them agency (choice between authorship and acknowledgment)
- Provide concrete next steps

### Don't:
- Assume permission will be granted
- Pressure them for quick responses
- Minimize their contributions
- Submit anything before getting explicit permission
- Burn bridges if they say no

### Timeline Suggestion:
- Initial email → Wait 2 weeks → Follow up
- If no response after follow-up → Wait 1 more week → Consider reaching out via different channel
- Total timeline before considering contingency plan: ~4-6 weeks
